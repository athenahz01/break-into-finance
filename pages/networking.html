Why This Module Is Make-or-Break

Technical preparation gets you through the interview. Networking gets you the interview. For non-target school candidates, networking accounts for 70-80% of all interview invitations. This module is the playbook nobody teaches in class — the exact scripts, timelines, and systems that convert strangers into advocates who pull your resume from the pile.

1

Memory Hook — "18 Months Out"

The golden rule: start networking 18 months before your target start date. If you want a Summer 2027 IB internship, begin networking in January 2026. If you want to lateral into PE by 2028, start building relationships now. Networking is a long game — people who start "when applications open" are already behind.

Cold Email Scripts That Work

Cold emailing is the #1 skill for non-target candidates. A well-crafted cold email has a 15-25% response rate. A generic one gets <2%. Here are battle-tested templates:

Template 1: The Alumni Outreach
Subject: [School Name] Student — Quick Question About [Group/Firm]

Hi [First Name],

I'm a [year] at [School] studying [major], and I came across your profile on LinkedIn — really impressed by your path from [their background detail] to [their current role] at [Firm].

I'm exploring a career in [IB/PE/VC] and would love to learn about your experience in [specific group or deal type]. Would you have 15-20 minutes for a quick call this week or next?

Completely understand if the timing doesn't work — I know how busy things get on the desk.

Thanks so much,
[Your Name]
[Phone] | [LinkedIn URL]
Template 2: The Non-Alumni / Cold Contact
Subject: Aspiring [Role] — [Specific Angle]

Hi [First Name],

I recently read about [Firm]'s work on [specific deal/report/news — do your homework]. As someone building a career in [area], I found [specific detail] particularly interesting.

I'm a [brief 1-line background]. I've been preparing for a career in [IB/PE] by [1-2 concrete things you've done: modeling course, relevant internship, CFA progress].

Would you be open to a brief 15-minute call? I'd love to hear how you think about [specific, thoughtful question about their work].

Best,
[Your Name]
Memory Hook — The 4 Rules of Cold Emails
Short · Specific · Sincere · Small ask

Short (under 120 words — they're reading on mobile). Specific (mention THEIR name, firm, group, a recent deal). Sincere (genuine curiosity, not a form letter). Small ask (15 minutes, not "can you get me a job"). If your email violates any of these four S's, rewrite it.

Most banking emails follow predictable formats. Try these patterns (in order of likelihood):

Last resort: LinkedIn InMail (lower response rate)

Coffee Chat Playbook

You got the call. Now what? A great coffee chat makes someone want to go to bat for you. A bad one wastes both your time and burns a contact. Here's the exact structure:

The 20-Minute Coffee Chat Structure

1

Minutes 0-2: Warm-Up

"Thanks so much for taking the time. I know you're busy." Brief personal connection (school, city, shared interest). Do NOT go over 2 minutes here.

2

Minutes 2-5: Their Story

"I'd love to hear how you ended up at [Firm]. What drew you to [their specific group]?" Let them talk. People love talking about themselves. Listen actively and ask follow-ups.

3

Minutes 5-12: Substantive Questions (3-4 max)

This is the meat. Ask smart, specific questions that show you've done your homework. Examples: "I noticed [Firm] advised on [deal]. What's it like working on a cross-border transaction like that?" or "How has AI changed the day-to-day work for analysts in your group?"

4

Minutes 12-15: Your Story (Brief)

When they ask about you (and they will), deliver your 60-second "Past → Present → Future" story. Be concise. Don't monologue.

5

Minutes 15-18: The Referral Ask (Subtle)

"Is there anyone else on the team or at the firm you'd recommend I speak with?" This is how you build your network exponentially. Never ask "can you refer me for the job" directly — let it happen organically after they've gotten to know you.

6

Minutes 18-20: Close & Thank

"This has been incredibly helpful — I really appreciate your time." End early if possible (they'll notice and appreciate it). Send a thank-you email within 2 hours.

Questions That Kill Your Chances
  • "What does your company do?" (shows zero research)
  • "What are the hours like?" (makes you look lazy)
  • "Can you refer me?" (too direct, too early)
  • "How much do you make?" (never ask this)
  • Anything you could Google in 30 seconds
Memory Hook — The 80/20 Rule of Coffee Chats

They should talk 80% of the time. You talk 20%. Your job is to ask great questions and listen — not to deliver a monologue about yourself. The biggest networking mistake is talking too much. When you talk less, they feel heard, and people advocate for people who make them feel heard.

Memory Hook — The LinkedIn Drip System

Think of LinkedIn like watering a plant: connect → engage → message → meet → follow up → repeat. Don't go straight from "connect" to "can I have a job." Engage with their posts 2-3 times first (like, thoughtful comment). Then send a message. The whole drip takes 2-4 weeks per contact, but the conversion rate is 3-5x higher than cold outreach alone.

The Follow-Up System

Most candidates send one email and give up. The ones who break in follow up systematically. Here's the system:

Post-Coffee-Chat Follow-Up Timeline

1

Same Day (within 2 hours): Thank-You Email

"Thanks again for the time today. I especially appreciated your insight on [specific thing they said]. Looking forward to staying in touch."

2

2-4 Weeks Later: Value-Add Touch

Send them something relevant — an article about their industry, a deal they might find interesting, or an update on something you discussed. "Saw this article on [topic we discussed] and thought of you."

3

When Applications Open: The Ask

"I wanted to let you know I'm submitting my application to [Firm] for the [Role]. Would you be willing to flag my resume or put in a good word? I'd really appreciate it." By now, they know you and want to help.

4

After Getting the Job: Close the Loop

Always tell them the outcome. People want to know they helped. "I wanted to share the great news — I accepted an offer at [Firm]. Your guidance was a huge part of making that happen."

Memory Hook — Networking is a CRM

Treat your networking like a sales pipeline. Keep a spreadsheet with: Name, Firm, Date of Contact, Last Follow-Up, Next Action, Notes. The candidates who break into finance from non-targets typically have 50-100+ contacts in their CRM, with 15-25 "warm" relationships at target firms. This is a numbers game with a relationship multiplier.

On-Cycle PE Recruiting: The Sprint

On-cycle PE recruiting is the most intense, compressed recruiting process in all of finance. If you're a first-year IB analyst targeting megafund PE, here's what you need to know:

How It Works

The On-Cycle Timeline

Headhunters (HSP, CPI, Oxbridge, SG Partners, Ratio Advisors) contact IB analysts as early as spring of their first year — sometimes just 6 months into the job. They'll email you to gauge interest, then schedule "first round" interviews that double as screens. If a fund likes you, you may receive an offer within 24-72 hours of your final round. Some processes complete in a single weekend.

Preparation Strategy

ComponentWhat to PrepareWhen to Start
LBO ModelingPaper LBO (2 min), full LBO from scratch (30-45 min), LBO with add-ons and complex debtMonth 1 of IB role
Deal ExperienceBe able to walk through 2-3 deals in extreme detail: your role, key issues, how you'd invest differentlyImmediately — take notes on every deal
Investment ThesisPrepare 1-2 polished investment pitches with entry valuation, value creation plan, and exit analysisMonth 3-4 of IB role
Headhunter RelationshipsRespond to every headhunter email. Be polite, professional, and responsive — they control accessDay 1
Fit / Why PE"I want longer-term involvement with companies and to be on the investing side of the table"Always
Memory Hook — On-Cycle Survival
Respond fast · Always be ready · Carry your pitch · Every deal matters

RACE: On-cycle PE is literally a race. Respond to headhunters within hours (not days). Always be ready — you could get a "come in tomorrow" call at any point. Carry your pitch — have your investment thesis memorized. Every deal matters — take detailed notes on every deal you work on because they'll ask about all of them.

The Non-Target Playbook

Not from Harvard, Wharton, or a "target school"? That's fine — but your strategy must be different. Here's the real talk on what works:

The Non-Target Hierarchy of Effort

1

Boutique / Middle-Market Banks First

Firms like Houlihan Lokey, William Blair, Harris Williams, Baird, Raymond James actively recruit from non-target schools. Get IB experience here first, then lateral to a bulge bracket or PE if that's your goal. This is the single most successful path for non-targets.

2

Network 3x Harder

Target school students get interviews through OCR (on-campus recruiting). You get them through 50+ coffee chats and cold emails. This is not optional — it's the price of admission.

3

Over-Index on Technicals

Target school students get the benefit of the doubt. You don't. Your technical answers need to be sharper, your models cleaner, your deal knowledge deeper. Prepare to the point where you can teach the material, not just answer questions about it.

4

Build Credentials Strategically

CFA Level 1 (signals commitment), financial modeling certificates (BIWS, WSP, CFI), relevant internships (even if unpaid or at small firms), and a polished personal project (a stock pitch, a mock LBO) all help fill the "pedigree gap."

Memory Hook — The Non-Target Equation

Non-target success = (2x networking) + (1.5x technical prep) + (1 compelling story) + (relentless follow-up). You can't change your school. But you can out-prepare, out-network, and out-hustle every target school student who's coasting on brand name alone. The firms that value this are the firms worth working for.

Memory Hook — The 50/15/5 Rule

To land a competitive finance role: send 50+ cold outreach emails, convert into 15+ substantive conversations, develop 5+ warm advocates who will pull for you when it matters. This ratio holds remarkably well across IB, PE, and consulting recruiting. It's a funnel — work the numbers.
